LUNG CANCER INTERACTIVE

1. The Invisible Enemy: Wildfire Smoke

Wildfire smoke contains toxic Particulate Matter (PM). PM2.5 particles are 30x smaller than human hair, bypassing defenses to lodge deep in lungs.

Long-term exposure introduces carcinogens, drastically increasing pulmonary disease risk.

Quick Check: Most dangerous particulate matter size?

2. How Smoke Damages Lung Cells

PM2.5 particles cause oxidative stress in airways and alveoli. This damages cell DNA leading to mutations and uncontrolled growth (cancer).

Toxins like PAHs and heavy metals directly attack genetic material, accelerating lung cancer development.

Quick Check: Cellular damage from toxins is called?

3. Protection and Prevention

Best protection is avoiding exposure. When air quality is poor, stay indoors with air filtration.

Firefighters MUST use certified respiratory protection (SCBA) during fire suppression, overhaul, and cleanup. Regular medical screenings are critical for early detection.
